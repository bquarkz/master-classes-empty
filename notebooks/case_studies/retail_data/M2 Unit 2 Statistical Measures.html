Introduction to Descriptive Statistics with Python¶

Measures of Central Tendency, Variation, Skewness and Correlation¶

Objectives¶

  • Understand descriptive statistics concepts using Python.
  • Learn how to calculate measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode).
  • Learn how to calculate measures of dispersion (variance, standard deviation, coefficient of variation).
  • Assess skewness of the distribution

Data Description¶

Columns Description Possible values
Retailer Retailer ID -
Zone Location of the retailer North, East, West, South (4)
Retailer_Age Number of years doing business with the company <=2, 2 to 5, >5 (3)
Perindex Index of performance based on sales, buying frequency, and buying recency positive values
Growth Annual sales growth Real values
NPS_Category Category indicating loyalty with the company Detractor, Passive, Promoter (3)

Trimmed mean (5% trimmed)¶

In Python, a 5% trimmed mean removes 5% of data from both ends, effectively trimming a total of 10% before calculating the mean.

In [7]:
trimmed_mean_growth = stats.trim_mean(data['Growth'], 0.05)
print(f"Trimmed mean Growth: {trimmed_mean_growth}")

Trimmed mean Growth: 4.943516483516483

Measures of Dispersion¶

Variance, Standard Deviation, Coefficient of Variation¶

In [9]:
variance_growth = data['Growth'].var()
std_growth = data['Growth'].std()
cv_growth = std_growth / data['Growth'].mean()

print(f"Variance of Growth: {variance_growth:.3f}")
print(f"Standard Deviation of Growth: {std_growth:3f}")
print(f"Coefficient of Variation (CV) of Growth: {cv_growth:.3f}")

Standard Deviation of Growth: 2.630961
Coefficient of Variation (CV) of Growth: 0.512
